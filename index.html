The Doctrine
NO ONE KNOWS WHAT'S COMING. Not the analysts. Not the algorithms. Not you. SO BUILD A PORTFOLIO THAT DOESN'T NEED TO.

The 33% strategy isn't about maximizing returns. It's about surviving every market condition with your wealth — and your sanity — intact. When stocks crash, gold and cash protect you. When inflation rages, gold surges. When everything booms, equities compound. You win by not losing.

The Three Pillars

Each one thrives in conditions that hurt the others. That's the whole point.
01
The Rock · Gold · 33%

Your Crisis Insurance

"Gold doesn't pay dividends. It pays in survival."

Stocks can go to zero. Bonds can default. Banks can fail. Gold cannot go bankrupt. It's the only asset with no counterparty risk — no company behind it, no government promise needed. For 5,000 years, gold has preserved wealth across civilizations, wars, and collapses. That track record deserves one-third of your portfolio.

When inflation eats your savings, gold eats the inflation. When central banks print trillions, gold becomes relatively scarcer. When the geopolitical map rewrites itself, investors flee to the one thing everyone trusts. Your gold allocation will feel boring — until the day it saves you.

During the 2008 crisis, gold rose 25% while stocks fell 55%
Gold has no CEO, no earnings, no quarterly report to disappoint
Simple access: GLD ETF, IAU ETF, or physical coins and bars
02
The Paper · Cash · 33%

Your Loaded Gun

"Everyone wants cash when panic sets in. You already have it."

Most people keep cash because they're afraid to invest it. This strategy keeps cash because it is the investment. Cash sitting in a high-yield savings account or Treasury bill earns a real yield — often 4–5% annually — while waiting for its true purpose.

That purpose? Dry powder. When everything is on fire and your neighbors are panic-selling their stocks at 40% off, your cash allocation turns into your most aggressive move. You deploy it into beaten-down equities during the annual rebalance, buying the most when everyone else has the least. Cash is not cowardice. It's preparation.

In deflationary crashes, cash gains purchasing power in real terms
T-Bills: backed by the US government, liquid within days
At 5% APY, your cash earns $500/yr per $10,000 invested
03
The Equity · Stocks · 33%

The Long Game

"Companies solve problems. That's what makes them worth owning forever."

The S&P 500 has returned roughly 10% annually since 1926. Through the Great Depression, World War II, a dozen recessions, a dot-com bust, a financial crisis, and a global pandemic — the long-run line goes up and to the right. Equities are the best wealth-creation engine ever invented.

The catch? The ride is violent. Stocks can — and do — drop 50%. Most people sell in terror and lock in their losses. By holding only one-third in equities, you mute the drawdown to a survivable dip. And by rebalancing annually, you automatically buy more shares when they're cheap and fewer when they're expensive. The discipline is built in.

S&P 500 has never lost money over any 20-year rolling period in history
A $1 invested in US equities in 1926 is worth over $10,000 today
Low-cost access: VOO, VTI, or SPY — expense ratios under 0.05%

What This Strategy Doesn't Promise — and Why That's the Point.

This strategy will not make you rich overnight. If stocks surge 40% and you hold only a third of your portfolio in them, you won't capture the full gain. That's an explicit trade-off, not a bug.

The goal is to never be catastrophically wrong. You will never have a year where you lose 50% of your net worth. You will never be the person who panic-sold at the bottom and watched the recovery happen without you.

Compound interest is the most powerful force in wealth-building. The strategy works by keeping you in the game long enough for compounding to work its slow, relentless magic. A portfolio that earns 8% every year for 30 years outperforms one that earns 15% for 10 years and then loses 60%.

Don't optimize for the best year. Optimize for the best decade.

§ The Annual Protocol

The Rebalancing Protocol

Once a year, you spend 20 minutes "fixing" the portfolio. This single act of discipline — selling the winner, buying the laggard — is the invisible engine of the entire strategy. It forces you to buy low and sell high, automatically, every year, without thinking.

01
Step One

Pick Your Day

Choose one date per year and calendar it. Your birthday. New Year's Day. January 15th. The date doesn't matter — the consistency does. This is the only day you are allowed to look at allocations with the intent to act. Every other day: do nothing, feel nothing, change nothing.

02
Step Two

Find the Winner

Open your brokerage. If stocks had a 50% year, they might now be 42% of your portfolio instead of 33%. If gold surged during a crisis, it might be 38%. Whoever is above 33.3% is your "winner." Congratulations — you're about to sell high on it.

03
Step Three

Reset. Done.

Sell enough of the winner to bring it back to 33.3%. Use those proceeds to buy the laggard until all three pillars sit at exactly one-third. You have now — without any prediction or genius — mechanically sold the most expensive asset and bought the cheapest one. Close the app. You're done for the year.
